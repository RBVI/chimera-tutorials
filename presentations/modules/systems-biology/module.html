Systems biology...is about putting together rather than taking apart, integration rather than reduction. It requires that we develop ways of thinking about integration that are as rigorous as our reductionist programmes, but different....It means changing our philosophy, in the full sense of the term (Denis Noble).

Systems Biology

  • Computationally driven
    • Modeling of biological systems
  • Data-driven
    • Heavy use of high-throughput techniques
    • Data drives hypotheses
    • Holistic vs reductionist
      • Looks for emergent properties
    • Requirements for visualizations and analytical tools are more exploratory than explanatory
      • Although explanatory tools are still needed to publish/present results

Systems Biology

  • Example high throughput techniques:
    • Expression profiling:
      • Gene expression microarray
      • RNAseq, scRNAseq
    • Protein-protein interaction:
      • Affinity Purification/Mass Spectometry
      • Yeast 2-hybrid
    • Proteomics:
      • Mass spectrometry protein profiling
      • Protein microarrays
    • Epigenetics:
      • CHiP-seq
    • Chromatin interactions:
      • 3C,4C,5C, and Hi-C
    • Genetic interactions:
      • E-MAPs, SGA

Systems Biology

  • Data growth
    • Scale for experiments of a given type (e.g. RNA expression) are growing dramatically
      • Split Pool Ligation-based Transcriptome sequencing (SPLiT-seq): ~100,000 cells
    • The number of types of experiments are also growing
    • Starting to look at time-dependent and condition-dependent data
    • There is an increasing demand for integration
      • Protein-protein interaction + expression
      • Transcriptomics + proteomics
      • Epigenetics + chromatin interactions
  • What tools do we have to cope with this growth?
    • Computational approaches: machine learning
    • → Exploratory visualizations
