Systems Biology

Systems biology...is about putting together rather than taking apart, integration rather than reduction. It requires that we develop ways of thinking about integration that are as rigorous as our reductionist programmes, but different....It means changing our philosophy, in the full sense of the term (Denis Noble).

Systems Biology

  • Data-driven
    • Heavy use of high-throughput techniques
  • Data (observation) drives hypotheses
  • Holistic vs reductionist
    • Looks for emergent properties
  • Visualizations and analytical tools are more exploratory than explanatory
    • Although explanatory tools are still needed to publish/present results

Systems Biology

  • Example high throughput techniques:
    • Expression profiling:
      • Gene expression microarray
      • RNAseq, scRNAseq
    • Protein-protein interaction:
      • Affinity Purification/Mass Spectometry
      • Yeast 2-hybrid
    • Proteomics:
      • Mass spectrometry protein profiling
      • Protein microarrays
    • Epigenetics:
      • CHiP-seq
      • 3C,4C,5C, and Hi-C
    • Genetic interactions:
      • E-MAPs, SGA
