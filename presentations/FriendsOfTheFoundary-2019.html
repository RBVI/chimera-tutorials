(Really) Big Data

  • # of atoms in average protein (~375 aa): 7.2 X 103
  • # of proteins in a cell: ~1 X 1010 (Milo, 2013)
  • # of lipids in a human cell: ~1 X 1010 (Rubakhin, et al. 2011)
  • # of metabolites in a human cell: ~1 X 108 (Matabolimics (EBI))
  • # of RNA Molecules in a human cell: ~1 X 108 (Matabolimics (EBI))
  • # of miRNA Molecules in a human cell: ~1 X 104 (Bissels, 2009)
  • # of molecular entities >>1 X 1040 (we skipped over carbohydrates, other small RNAs, etc.)
  • # of cells in a human: ~3.7 X 1013 (Bianconi, et al. 2013)
  • Potential interactions: ~3.7 X 1053 squared

(Really) Big Data

But wait, there's more!

  • Connections aren't static. They vary based on:
    • time (development, aging)
    • environmental conditions
    • disease (e.g. infection, somatic mutation)

Challenge

Understanding biological connections is critical to:

  • understanding biological processes
  • understanding the impacts of industrialization
  • understanding impacts of climate change
  • understanding (and combating) disease

Challenge

Understanding biological connections will take:

  • new computational algorithms and approaches to deal with massive scale
  • new approaches to visualization to support in the interpretation of data
  • new collaborations between biology, medical science, computer science, and mathematics
